Getting Started

Quickly getting into a classroom.

Requirements

1. VR devices set up. (You can successfully launch any game.) You should also have pengram.exe ready on your computer.

2. AR device set up. You should have Pengram application already installed on your AR device. Make sure to also have a Vuforia marker ready printed on a piece of paper. (You can download it from Here. Black and white is fine.)

3. Make sure both AR and VR users are in same WiFi network, otherwise the video streaming from Hololens to VR side won’t work in current version. If both AR and VR are connected to a corporate network, there is a high chance that the video stream might still not work. Safest way is to connect to same local router’s WiFi network for both devices.


 

Getting started

AR, trainee side

1. Air-tap Pengram AR Demo program installed on Hololens

2. Wait after Unity logo disappears and you can see a "READY" button in front of you (this should take few seconds, make sure you fix your gaze until "READY" button appears). Now tell the VR user (instructor) to enter the room. (Make sure the READY button is there before you signal VR user, but don’t click the button yet).
[Note: If Unity logo doesn’t appear and you go directly to a Pengram demo (signaled by seeing a READY button), perform a Bloom gesture, close the Pengram Demo tile, and launch Pengram Demo again.]

3. You can check that the instructor has entered by
- the appearance of a triagle, which points you to the location of the instructor's avatar.
- the audio connection. (make sure each of you can hear the voice from the other side )

4. Look at provided Vuforia marker and place it on the table, confirm that a virtual motor appears. (This procedure is to tell the VR side where the motor should be.)

5. Move the physical motor to match the position and rotation of the virtual motor. Alternatively you can move the virtual motor to align with the physical motor by moving the marker.

6. Once you feel physical motor and virtual motor matched at its pose, look at "READY" button (A cursor should appear on the button) and do air-tap. Successful tap is signaled by "READY" button disappearing. By now, you can toss Vuforia marker as we don’t need it anymore.

7. CONGRATULATIONS! You are ready!

VR, Instructor side

1. Launch Pengram VR client and click login, there is no need to type password or username for this demo. (Also for the demo, make sure that trainess is already in the classroom before you login.)

2. After "Loading.." Disappears, check if there is room "2018" on screen, if there is, type “2018” in "room name" field, (Ignore "username" for now) and click "join room".

3. After a few seconds, the desktop screen will be black. Now put on your headset, you will be in the classroom. You can make sure by
- the sky background
- your virtual hands.
- the audio connection. (make sure each of you can hear the voice from the other side )

4. Look around and find your trainee's head avatar and the virtual motor. Fly to the virtual motor and begins. (If you are in local network, make sure to take the video tablet with you.) You should appear higher than avatar of the AR user, fly down by looking at avatar of the VR user and push right hand joystick forward.

5. Now you can interact with all models and tools using Pengram's features. Here are some examples (For detailed instructions on the features, refer to the features section):
a. Scale yourself for more precise grabbing
b. Drawing annotations using lines and arrows

6. CONGRATULATIONS! You are ready!

Known Issues

Since the version we are providing are still in early development, followings are some bugs and issues we are aware and are currently fixing. We also provide a temporary fix below every issue.

Issue: Avatar of either player stops moving
Solution: We found this issue with one player drops after certain period of time. Unfortunately we don’t have a solution for it yet other than restarting the session

Issue: Avatar of either player stops moving
Solution: We found this issue with one player drops after certain period of time. Unfortunately we don’t have a solution for it yet other than restarting the session

Issue: It’s hard to perfectly align the virtual object with real object
Solution: We are currently developing a more advanced computer vision algorithm to automatically align objects. For currently demo purpose, due to limitation of the hardware, manual alignment with Vuforia is the best way so far but does not reflect product feature in the long run.

Issue: When you scale down to quite small (2% of original size), you can’t select items on menu on left hand
Solution: A bug with Unity. Please try not to scale to that small if you wish to use drawing tools.

Issue: When second VR person join in the room, even if the second VR person is in same WiFi network as the AR user, he or she still cannot see the video stream from AR user.
Solution: We are developing a better video streaming technology using third paty software to bring a more stable experience.

Issue: See black screen when launching VR executable.
Solution: Put the VR headset on. This is because the if proximity sensor of Oculus or Vive detect no one is wearing the headset, it won’t display anything to conserve power.
